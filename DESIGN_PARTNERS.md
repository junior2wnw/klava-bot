# Design Partner Program

`Klava` is looking for a small number of design partners who have real local-machine workflows worth productizing.

This is not a generic "try the app" request. The best fit is a team or operator with a repeated, expensive workflow that sits between chat, shell work, approvals, secrets, and machine changes.

## Good fits

- consultants who repeatedly fix or migrate developer machines;
- internal IT or platform teams with risky workstation tasks;
- founders or engineers who move projects between providers, stacks, or local environments;
- support operators who need transcripts, approvals, and support bundles instead of ad-hoc screen sharing;
- power users who want a local-first agent with an audit trail instead of a pure chat product.

## What Klava is aiming to prove

- one desktop agent can own the full local work loop;
- risky actions should go through explicit approvals;
- secrets should stay outside the transcript;
- high-value machine workflows should be typed and reviewable;
- a product can turn "please help with my machine" into a repeatable operator surface.

## What a design partner gets

- direct influence on roadmap and workflow prioritization;
- a faster path for the workflow that matters to you;
- a chance to shape packaging, approvals, and operator UX around real use cases;
- optional public case-study visibility if the outcome is strong and both sides want it.

## What Klava needs from you

- a real workflow, not a hypothetical prompt;
- concrete failure modes, constraints, and success criteria;
- willingness to explain how the task is handled today;
- clarity on urgency, stakes, and whether there is budget behind the problem.

## Apply

Open a public issue with the design-partner template:

- https://github.com/junior2wnw/klava-bot/issues/new?template=design_partner.md

Do not post secrets, credentials, customer data, or private infrastructure details in public issues.

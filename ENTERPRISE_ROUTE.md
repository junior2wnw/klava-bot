# Enterprise Route

This file is the blunt commercial strategy, not the product narrative.

## Reality

No `README`, badge, or GitHub view count produces a `$10M` contract.

Very large contracts usually come from one of these routes:

- `enterprise standardization`: one painful workflow becomes part of how a large org operates;
- `channel or OEM distribution`: a larger platform, MSP, or endpoint vendor decides the workflow belongs in its own motion.

Everything else is lead generation.

## The wedge

Do not sell `Klava` as "an AI desktop app."

Sell it as:

> an approval-gated local workflow system for expensive machine work that already costs real money when humans do it manually.

## Best buyers

- Head of Platform Engineering
- Director of Workplace Engineering
- Director of End User Computing
- MSP owner / COO / VP Services
- Security-conscious IT leader with approval and audit requirements

## Priority routes

### Route A: OEM / channel / platform partners

This is the route with the biggest upside because one integration or distribution deal can touch many operators and many endpoints.

Targets to watch:

- `Automox OEM`: https://www.automox.com/partners/oem
- `Jamf Managed Service Providers`: https://www.jamf.com/partners/managed-service-providers/
- `ConnectWise RMM/RPA motion`: https://www.connectwise.com/company/press/releases/next-gen-rmm-rpa-solutions-deliver-efficiency-value
- `ServiceNow digital employee experience`: https://www.servicenow.com/content/dam/servicenow-assets/public/en-us/doc-type/resource-center/solution-brief/sb-digital-end-user-experience.pdf

Why this route matters:

- these companies already sell automation, support, compliance, or endpoint operations;
- they already own budgets, distribution, or operator surfaces;
- they care about repeatable workflows, not demo prompts.

### Route B: MSP and operator-led lighthouse customers

This is the fastest way to get proof because the buyer already feels the pain repeatedly.

Targets to watch:

- `Action1 MSP partner program`: https://www.action1.com/msp-partner/
- `NinjaOne endpoint management`: https://www.ninjaone.com/endpoint-management/
- `Atera IT automations`: https://www.atera.com/it-automations/
- `Tanium autonomous endpoint management`: https://www.tanium.com/autonomous-endpoint-management/

Why this route matters:

- MSPs and high-volume support operators repeat the same workflow often enough to justify productization;
- one successful pilot can expand from one runbook to many;
- the operator already understands the dollar cost of manual work.

### Route C: Enterprise platform and workplace teams

This route is slower, but it creates the strongest case study if the workflow is ugly enough and the team size is large enough.

Best fit:

- engineering orgs with `50+` developers on Windows and macOS;
- platform teams doing repeated provider or environment migrations;
- workplace teams supporting developer endpoints with strict approvals and audit requirements.

## What to sell first

The first paid motion should be one of these:

- developer workstation recovery and rebuild;
- risky local migration with rollback and approval requirements;
- repeated endpoint remediation workflow;
- support-bundle and machine triage workflow for high-cost incidents.

## What not to sell first

- a generic AI assistant;
- "install this cool desktop app";
- broad enterprise transformation language;
- anything that depends on the buyer imagining the ROI for you.

## 30-day operating plan

1. Close `2` paid pilots around one narrow workflow category.
2. Turn each pilot into a case study with hard numbers: time saved, escalation reduction, or incident recovery speed.
3. Use those case studies to approach OEM/channel/platform targets with a stronger story than "interesting GitHub repo."
4. Keep every outbound message anchored in workflow economics, approvals, and auditability.

## Required message discipline

Lead with:

- repeated expensive workflow;
- approval model;
- operator clarity;
- faster recovery or migration;
- proof that the system leaves receipts.

Do not lead with:

- agents in general;
- model quality in general;
- future multimodal plans;
- open-source ideology by itself.

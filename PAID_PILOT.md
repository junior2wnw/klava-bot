# Klava Paid Pilot

`Klava` is not looking for generic app testers.

It is looking for one expensive local-machine workflow that is painful enough to justify a paid pilot.

## Best fit

- MSPs and MSSPs that repeat the same support or remediation workflow across many customers;
- internal IT, workplace engineering, or platform teams that repeatedly repair or rebuild developer machines;
- engineering orgs running risky local migrations that need approvals, transcripts, and rollback context;
- consultants who already get paid to fix the same local-machine mess over and over again.

## Strong pilot candidates

- developer workstation recovery and rebuild;
- local project migration between providers, backends, or environments;
- approval-gated terminal workflows with audit trail requirements;
- support-bundle collection and machine triage for hard incidents;
- repeated endpoint remediation steps that currently live across docs, shell history, and tribal memory.

## What the pilot should produce

- one real workflow turned into a repeatable operator surface;
- explicit approval points for risky actions;
- transcript and supportability artifacts another engineer can review;
- rollback and recovery notes where practical;
- a before/after measure tied to time, failure rate, escalation load, or operator effort.

## Suggested commercial shape

This is the commercial shape Klava should optimize for. Exact pricing depends on scope, security review, and deployment model.

1. `Discovery`: 1 week, scoped workflow, security constraints, success metric, and rollout boundary.
2. `Pilot`: 4-6 weeks, one painful workflow productized into typed steps, approvals, and operator records.
3. `Rollout`: extend the same operating model to more workflows, more operators, or more environments.

Suggested ranges:

- discovery: `$5k-$15k`
- pilot: `$25k-$100k`
- rollout: `six figures+`
- OEM/channel path after proof: `seven or eight figures`

## What large deals actually require

A very large contract does not come from random GitHub traffic.

It usually comes from one of two routes:

- a painful workflow gets standardized across a large team or organization;
- a channel, OEM, or platform partner decides the workflow should be distributed through their own product or services motion.

## Open a pilot request

Use the paid-pilot intake:

- https://github.com/junior2wnw/klava-bot/issues/new?template=paid_pilot.md

If the workflow is budgeted, say that plainly and include:

- the operator team;
- endpoint or user count;
- the workflow that is currently expensive;
- approval, logging, and compliance constraints;
- decision timeline and budget range.
